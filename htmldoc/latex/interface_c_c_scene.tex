\subsection{Detailed Description}
\hyperlink{interface_c_c_scene}{C\-C\-Scene} is a subclass of \hyperlink{class_c_c_node}{C\-C\-Node} that is used only as an abstract concept.

\hyperlink{interface_c_c_scene}{C\-C\-Scene} an \hyperlink{class_c_c_node}{C\-C\-Node} are almost identical with the difference that \hyperlink{interface_c_c_scene}{C\-C\-Scene} has its anchor point (by default) at the center of the screen.

For the moment \hyperlink{interface_c_c_scene}{C\-C\-Scene} has no other logic than that, but in future releases it might have additional logic.

It is a good practice to use and \hyperlink{interface_c_c_scene}{C\-C\-Scene} as the parent of all your nodes.
